Welcome to Earth!

Earth is our home planet is the third planet from the Sun, and the fifth largest planet. It's the only place we know of inhabited by living things.

About Earth

Earth is the third planet from the Sun and the only astronomical object known to harbor life. About 29.2% of Earth's surface is land consisting of continents and islands. The remaining 70.8% is covered with water, mostly by oceans, seas, gulfs, and other salt-water bodies, but also by lakes, rivers, and other freshwater, which together constitute the hydrosphere. It is the only planet in our solar system that has liquid water on its surface, which is essential for life as we know it. Earth has a diverse range of ecosystems, climates, and landscapes, from lush forests to arid deserts, and from towering mountains to vast oceans. The planet's atmosphere is composed of 78% nitrogen, 21% oxygen, and trace amounts of other gases, which support life and protect us from harmful solar radiation. Earth has a rich history, with evidence of human civilization dating back thousands of years.

Nearest Planets

Venus and Mars are Earth's nearest planetary neighbors.

Distance from Earth

The distance between Earth and other planets varies depending on their orbits. For example, the distance to Mars can be as close as 54.6 million kilometers.

Interesting Facts about Earth

  • Earth is the only planet not named after a mythological being.
  • It takes approximately 365.25 days for Earth to orbit the Sun, which is why we have a leap year every four years.
  • The highest point on Earth is Mount Everest, which stands at 8,848 meters (29,029 feet) above sea level.
  • The Earth's rotation is gradually slowing down, which means that days are getting longer over time.
  • Earth's atmosphere protects us from meteoroids and harmful solar radiation.

Solar System Overview

The Solar System consists of the Sun and all the celestial bodies that are bound to it by gravity, including eight planets, their moons, dwarf planets, asteroids, comets, and meteoroids. The four inner planets (Mercury, Venus, Earth, and Mars) are rocky, while the four outer planets (Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus, and Neptune) are gas giants.
